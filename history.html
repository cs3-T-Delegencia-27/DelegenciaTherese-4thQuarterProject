Biophysics is an interdisciplinary science branch dedicated to investigating biological processes using the

approaches of physics. Biophysics research has aided in illness prevention and treatment, medicine discovery, and

the creation of technology that will allow humanity to live more sustainably while also protecting the changing

environment.



Biophysics is a Bridge in Various Sciences



The Father of Biophysics

His Contributions & Tenets

- Kenneth Cole, ph.d. is regarded as the father of biophysics. He was the world's foremost expert on the biophysics of the nervous system. His research into nerve impulse transmission, particularly electrical resistance in squid nerve cells, is credited with paving the way for significant developments in neurophysiology in the 1930s and 1940s.



- Dr. Cole developed the unique voltage clamping technique, which helped pave the way for Alan L. Hodgkin and Andrew F. Huxley's formulation of the "sodium theory," for which they received the Nobel Prize in 1947. He was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1956 and was awarded the National Medal of Science in 1967.



Biophysics in DNA & Genetic Sequencing

Its Foundations

- Working at the intersection of two dissimilar sciences - physics and biology - can be challenging. Physicists must thoroughly immerse themselves in the complicated, even messy, realm of biology. Although DNA sequencing has provided us with the whole library of the human genome, the majority of the books are written in a language we do not understand.



- That's because only 2% of all human DNA codes for proteins, with the remaining 98% serving a murkier or no purpose at all. Without this insight, the functional nature of DNA remains unknown. However, by applying theoretical physics concepts, researchers are beginning to establish a comprehensive understanding of how genes are regulated.



Beginnings in Founding Biophysics

The Masterminds & Their Works

- Biophysics is a very recent discipline of science, emerging as a distinct subfield in the early to mid-twentieth century. However, the groundwork for the study of biophysics was built much earlier, in the nineteenth century, by a group of physiologists in Berlin.



- Hermann von Helmholtz, Emil DuBois-Reymond, Ernst von Brücke, and Carl Ludwig were among the physiologists who studied in Berlin. In 1856, Adolf Fick, one of Ludwig's students, wrote the first biophysics textbook. However, physics technology had not yet evolved enough to examine lifeforms in great detail, such as at the molecular level.



Biophysical Innovations in Radiology

Its Methods & Workings

- Since its inception, biophysicists have created advanced diagnostic imaging techniques such as MRIs, CT scans, and PET scans.



- Biophysics remains critical to the development of even safer, faster, and more precise technology to improve medical imaging and teach us more about the body's inner workings, as demonstrated by the establishment of biophysical imaging and its techniques, which range from advanced fluorescence strategies to scattering and multi-photon imaging.
