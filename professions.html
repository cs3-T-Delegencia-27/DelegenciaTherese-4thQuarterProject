Professions

A biophysics degree prepares you for a wide range of career opportunities. Biophysics graduates have virtually

limitless job opportunities. Here are some career options for the said discipline of study:




List of Biophysics Career Options




Laboratory Technician

Laboratory workers carry out a variety of tasks in a laboratory. They also maintain laboratory equipment, support lead scientists with their responsibilities, collect and analyze samples or substances, record observations, and plan laboratory tests in accordance with established testing procedures.



Radiologist

Radiologists use medical imaging technologies such as x-rays and ultrasounds to diagnose and treat a wide range of injuries and illnesses. They also assess a patient's medical history, interpret test results, collaborate with other medical professionals, and create extensive reports on their findings.



Research Associate

Research associates track the progress of research initiatives and conduct various tests and studies. They also carry out sophisticated experiments, collect, prepare, and analyze specimens, as well as conduct research and analysis under the supervision of a senior researcher.



Microbiologist

Microbiologists study minute creatures like bacteria and algae. They identify these organisms and track their growth to better understand their properties and how to treat a wide range of infectious disorders.



Environmental Scientist

Environmental scientists recognize both environmental and human-related risks. They collect environmental data for scientific study, analyze samples and surveys to identify environmental dangers, and devise strategies to help prevent or control environmental problems.



Ecologist

Ecologists investigate the interactions of living species, such as humans, with their environment. They examine environmental sites, collect specimens, monitor erosion control, analyze data using statistical models, and conduct field, laboratory, and theoretical research.



Chemist

Chemists investigate the qualities and composition of stuff. They conduct experiments with lab equipment, test solutions, analyze substances, do quality control tests, and assess and report test results. Chemists also decide how to improve procedures and equipment.

Pharmacologist

Pharmacologists create new pharmaceuticals through adequate study and testing. They discover potential adverse effects of these medications, assess the safety and efficacy of newly produced drugs, interpret data, make suggestions based on their findings, and supervise employees.
